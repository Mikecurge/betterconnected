Why schools are different

A school estate is not an office with classrooms attached.

Boarding houses that never close. Kitchens, pools and sports halls with demand profiles that look nothing like the timetable. Listed buildings with legacy metering. Term time consumption cliffs that confuse standard load profiling. And governance that rightly expects full visibility of the options and the reasoning before anything is signed.

Most energy firms treat a school as a small business with a large bill. We treat it as what it is: a multi-site estate with charitable governance, budget cycles set around the academic year and no tolerance for surprises in the operating budget.

For trusts and school groups the complexity multiplies. Procurement, renewal timing and reporting need to stay aligned across sites that sit under one ownership structure but operate day to day as separate schools. We manage exactly that structure today, in practice rather than in theory.

Case studies

Named schools. Real numbers.

Blundell's School, Tiverton, Devon

A full multi-site energy tender covering 29 gas supplies and 8 electricity supplies across the school's estate. Each site was assessed individually and compared across ten suppliers and three term lengths rather than treated as a single blanket renewal.

£141,392 combined saving
The school proceeded on a 24 month term, securing a saving of £80,470 on gas with Crown Gas and Power and £60,922 on electricity with Ecotricity against the previous cost base. The school's leadership had full visibility of the options and the reasoning behind the recommended route before anything was signed off.
Newman Schools group: Arnold Lodge School & Stratford Preparatory School

Better Connected manages energy supplies across both schools within the Newman Schools group, an independent two school group under common ownership spanning Leamington Spa and Stratford upon Avon. An 8 non Half Hourly electricity and 4 gas supply arrangement at Arnold Lodge, and 3 non Half Hourly electricity and 2 gas supplies at Stratford Preparatory.

Group structure, managed in practice
Procurement, renewal timing and reporting kept aligned across sites that sit under one ownership structure but operate day to day as separate schools. The same complexity a multi academy trust presents, handled in practice rather than in theory.
Yarlet School, Staffordshire

A day and boarding prep school on 2 Half Hourly electricity and 2 gas supplies. Boarding settings carry consumption around the clock, which makes metering accuracy and contract structure matter more, not less, at smaller scale.

Half Hourly, managed properly
Half Hourly supplies bring DUoS band exposure, capacity charges and data collection arrangements that reward active management. Yarlet's supplies are managed under the same advisory model as our largest estates.

Common questions

What bursars ask us first.

Do you have experience with schools like ours?

We manage energy for four independent schools today: Blundell's School in Tiverton, Arnold Lodge School in Leamington Spa, Stratford Preparatory School in Stratford upon Avon and Yarlet School in Staffordshire. Between them that covers day and boarding settings, prep and senior age ranges, Half Hourly and non Half Hourly metering and a two school group under common ownership.

What could we save?

The honest answer is that it depends on your estate and your starting position. The Blundell's tender delivered a combined saving of over £141,000 across gas and electricity. Smaller estates see smaller absolute numbers and the same discipline.

How are you paid?

Through an all inclusive advisory retainer built into the unit rate, active for the full life of the contract and disclosed in writing before anything is signed. The full model is in the Service Commitment. We also publish how this industry's commissions work.

We are a trust with multiple academies. Can you handle that?

The Newman Schools group is exactly that structure at independent scale: two schools, one ownership, separate day to day operations. Procurement, renewal timing and reporting stay aligned across the group. The same model extends to multi academy trusts.
